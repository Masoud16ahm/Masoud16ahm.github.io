About
------
I am a researcher in computational mechanics specialising in the development of mathematical and numerical models for complex engineering systems. My work focuses on finite element methods, fracture mechanics, multiscale modelling, and the integration of probabilistic and data-driven approaches such as uncertainty quantification and Bayesian inference. I am particularly interested in applying mathematical and numerical modelling, and modern statistical techniques to tackle real-world engineering and biomedical problems.

I am currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the [Department of Mathematics](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/mathematical-physical-sciences/maths) at [University College London (UCL)](https://www.ucl.ac.uk/), where I joined in February 2025 after completing my PhD in Civil Engineering at the [University of Glasgow](https://www.gla.ac.uk/). My current research focuses on predictive modelling of vertebral fracture risk in metastatic bone disease as part of the EPSRC-funded [OncoEng](https://oncoeng.org/) project.


Research Interests
------
* Computational mechanics and finite element methods  
* Fracture mechanics and failure modelling  
* Multiphysics modelling of materials (e.g., batteries and composites)  
* Uncertainty quantification and probabilistic modelling
* Scientific machine learning for engineering systems  
* Bayesian inference and statistical learning


OncoEng
------
The [OncoEng](https://oncoeng.org/) project develops computational models for predicting vertebral fracture risk in patients with metastatic bone disease. The project integrates medical imaging, finite element modelling, and biomechanical analysis to estimate the structural competence of cancer-affected vertebrae. The ultimate goal is to provide clinicians with predictive tools that can support treatment planning and improve patient outcomes.

Within this project, I contribute to developing a computational model for predicting vertebral fracture risk in metastatic bone disease. The aim is to provide clinicians with tools that support treatment planning by identifying vertebrae that are most vulnerable to fracture.
