Incompressible hyperelastic materials, commonly found in elastomers, biological tissues, and various engineering applications, exhibit nonlinear, reversible behaviour under large deformations. A primary challenge in modelling these materials is addressing numerical instabilities such as locking. This study explores the finite element (FE) modelling of incompressible hyperelastic materials using a classic one-field and three-field mixed formulation. We explore four different approaches for dimensionality treatment, including mathematically 2D, planner assumptions encompassing plane-strain and plane-stress, as well as fully 3D models. While 2D, plane-strain, and 3D models are well-established for hyperelastic materials, the plane stress assumption has often been overlooked in previous research. To validate model accuracy, simulations are conducted on benchmark problems widely recognized in the field, with a comparative analysis conducted among the different approaches. While the mixed-formulation is used to address incompressibility in 2D, plane-strain, and 3D models, the classic one-field FE model with a plane-stress assumption avoids locking problems.
